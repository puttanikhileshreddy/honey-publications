Peer Review Process

Peer review is a process to appraise the quality of a manuscript before it is published. It is a positive process. When a manuscript is sent to a journal, the editors check if it meets the criterion of submission and is sent to the review team of that particular journal to assess the quality, originality and significance of that manuscript. Peer review process provides valuable feedback so that the researchers can revise and improve their paper quality before publishing. Out of three main types of peer review, Honey team uses the double-blind process where the reviewers don’t know the author and the author don’t know the reviewer of their article.

After peer reviewed, articles become

  • More robust:Peer reviewers may figure out the gaps in the paper that requires more explanation or additional experiments.
  • Easier to read:If paper is difficult to understand, reviewers can suggest changes.
  • More useful:Peer reviewers also consider the importance of the paper to others in the same field.
